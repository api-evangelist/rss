Learn more here: https://rss.com/mediakit New Feature 📼 Added Support for Alternate Enclosure We love innovation in podcasting, especially the kind that makes our medium more open and accessible. We adopted Podcasting 2.0 features like Transcripts and Chapters early on, and later they became mainstream with support from Apple. We hope Alternate Enclosure is on a similar path. 
We have added support for the Alternate Enclosure under Settings → Power Ups. This allows you to attach alternative media files to the same episode using externally hosted URLs. For instance, you can provide lightweight audio files alongside your standard MP3. Supporting apps such as PodLP, a popular podcast app for feature phones with 10M+ installs, can automatically choose the lowest bitrate audio format, improving playback on slower networks and devices with limited resources. You can also attach other formats, including HLS video streams, as an alternative to the primary audio file. While major platforms like Apple Podcasts and Spotify do not support Alternate Enclosure yet, it is already implemented in a number of independent apps and we expect broader adoption to follow over time. Improvement 🤖 API Updates Since launching our public API last December, we have been steadily improving it behind the scenes. Today, we have updated several endpoints to a new version for better performance and reliability. Also, we are seeing great momentum: more podcasters are using our API to automate their workflows, and more third-party tools are integrating with RSS.com, from Zapier to apps that enable one-click episode publishing. Improvement 🍏 Apple Subscriptions: Short-Term Early Access Now Available Apple Podcasts Subscriptions is a low-effort way to monetize your podcast with enormous reach: iPhone alone represents roughly one quarter of the global active smartphone base, and subscribing is native with a single tap inside the Apple Podcasts app. Until now, Apple Early Access Episodes on RSS.com were geared toward longer windows (1 to 4 weeks). We have now added short-term Early Access options of 3 or 5 days, which are perfect for weekly shows and time-sensitive releases where a full week (or more) feels too long. We are also working on a new subscription model. Stay tuned 🙂 Improvement 📆 Improved Analytics Date Selector See the bigger picture. You can now select custom date ranges of up to 365 days in your Analytics dashboard, making it easier to track long-term trends, measure growth, and compare performance over time.
